About

Hi! My name is Lasya!

I'm a double-major in neuroscience and computer science with a minor in chemistry! I love to code - recently, I've been spending a lot of time doing mobile development and front-end work!

Outside of computer science, I love to read, write and travel! I recently visited Spain, France and Italy and had an incredible time. My biggest takeaway was that everyone should see Sagrada Familia at least once in their lifetime - it is truly a marvel of architechture, religion and culture. There are very few things that compare to its beauty!

I also have a passion for photography and art so I've been appointed the token photographer amongst my friends! Currently, I'm taking a feature writing class within the Hussman school at UNC and having a blast getting to become a better journalist!

Some fun things I've worked on outside of class: Monsoon and SHAC. Monsoon is UNC's premier South-Asian literary magazine that I ran for three years. I loved getting to bring South-Asian representation to the literary world; we published everything from couture photoshoots to human rights pieces. Getting to connect with my community and help spotlight South-Asian talent is an experience that I will treasure.

I also had a chance to help run the undergraduate part of SHAC - a free medical clinic that allows residents of Orange Country to access quality medical care for free. I loved getting to make a difference in my community and learn more about the healthcare system. The work that SHAC does for the community is critical and I'm grateful to have had a chance to be a part of a team that could make that happen.
